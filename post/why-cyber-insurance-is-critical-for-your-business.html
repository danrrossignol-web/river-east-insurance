Everyday Cyber Threats

1. Phishing Attacks: An employee clicks a link in a "trusted" vendor email. Suddenly, credentials are stolen and your systems are compromised.
2. Ransomware: Your files are encrypted and held for a $20,000 ransom. Without a backup or insurance, your doors stay closed indefinitely.
3. Data Breach: Customer credit card info or social security numbers are leaked. You are now legally required to notify them and provide monitoring.
4. Social Engineering: A criminal impersonates a high-ranking executive, tricking your finance team into a fraudulent wire transfer.
