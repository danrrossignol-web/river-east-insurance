Understanding Your Coverage

Liability & Medical Protection

Bodily Injury Liability This is the foundation of your protection. If you are at fault in an accident that injures others, this covers their medical bills and lost wages. High limits are essential to protect your personal assets and future earnings from lawsuits.
Property Damage Liability Covers the cost of damage you cause to someone else's property, such as their vehicle, a fence, or a guardrail.
Uninsured & Underinsured Motorist Crucial in Connecticut. If you are hit by a driver who has no insuranceâ€”or state minimum limits that won't cover your billsâ€”this coverage steps in to pay for your medical expenses.
Medical Payments Regardless of who is at fault, this covers reasonable medical expenses for you and your passengers in the event of an accident.

Vehicle & Asset Protection

Collision Coverage Pays to repair or replace your vehicle if you hit another car or object (like a tree or pole), or if your car rolls over. This usually comes with a deductible you choose.
Comprehensive Coverage Often called "Other than Collision." It covers damage from fire, theft, vandalism, falling objects (tree limbs), and contact with animals like deer.
Loan/Lease Gap Coverage Cars depreciate quickly. If your car is totaled and you owe more on the loan than the car is worth, Gap coverage pays the difference so you aren't paying for a car you can no longer drive.
Towing & Rental Reimbursement Peace of mind for the unexpected. Covers the cost of towing your disabled vehicle and pays for a rental car while yours is being repaired after a covered claim.
