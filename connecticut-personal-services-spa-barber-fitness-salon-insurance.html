Essential Coverage for Salons

Professional Liability

Also called "Salon E&O" or "Treatment Coverage." This is critical. It covers you if a service you perform causes injury (chemical burns, cuts, infections) or fails to deliver promised results.

  • Chemical Burns & Reactions
  • Accidental Cuts
  • Failure to deliver results

General Liability

Protects against "slip and fall" accidents in your shop. With high foot traffic and wet floors near shampoo bowls, this is a frequent claim area.

  • Slips, Trips & Falls
  • Damage to Customer Property
  • Advertising Injury

Commercial Property

Your chairs, sinks, tanning beds, and lasers are expensive. This covers your equipment, furniture, and improvements (build-outs) against fire, theft, or water damage.

  • Equipment Breakdown
  • Tenant Improvements
  • Inventory (Shampoos/Dyes)

Common Salon Insurance Questions

Does my policy cover Med Spa services?

Standard salon policies exclude invasive procedures like Botox, fillers, or laser hair removal. You need a specialized Med Spa policy for these medical-grade treatments.

I work from home. Is my equipment covered?

Homeowners insurance has very low limits for business property ($500-$2,500). It typically excludes business liability entirely. You need a home-based business endorsement or a BOP.

What if an employee steals from a client?

"Employee Dishonesty" coverage can protect you if a staff member steals cash or property from the business or a customer.

Is sexual abuse/molestation coverage needed?

For massage therapists and spas, this is a critical (though uncomfortable) coverage to consider. False allegations can destroy a reputation and finances without defense coverage.
